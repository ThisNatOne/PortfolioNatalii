Selected stories

Real-world QA beyond the test plan

Selected examples from my QA career showing how I work when quality depends on ownership, communication, technical judgement, and clear release decisions.

01

Production Incident & Recovery

Restoring critical services under pressure

Situation: A critical production outage affected platform availability and customer data access, requiring coordinated recovery across infrastructure and engineering teams.

Action: I supported troubleshooting, validated recovery steps, checked user-facing behaviour after each change, and helped coordinate progress under time pressure.

Impact: I contributed to restoring functionality on three out of four affected nodes, making approximately 75% of customer data accessible again while the remaining recovery work continued.

02

Delivery & Team Coordination

Bridging quality, planning, and execution

Situation: As the primary QA engineer, I worked close to both delivery planning and hands-on validation throughout the software lifecycle.

Action: I analysed requirements, joined solution reviews, supported proof-of-concept demos, created user guides, coordinated sprint planning and closure, and kept Kanban work visible.

Impact: Acting between business and technical perspectives helped the team stay aligned on priorities, deadlines, and the quality evidence needed before release.

03

Automation for Faster Releases

Reducing risk through automation

Situation: Repetitive manual checks were slowing release readiness and taking time away from deeper exploratory testing.

Action: I built a TypeScript and Playwright automation framework focused on critical business workflows, high-risk journeys, stable coverage, and fast feedback before deployment.

Impact: The suite reduced manual verification effort and created more space for risk assessment, exploratory testing, and production validation.

04

Sole QA Ownership

Owning quality from idea to production

Situation: Within a seven-person delivery team, I was the sole QA responsible for quality across planning, testing, release preparation, production validation, and incident response.

Action: I balanced hands-on testing, automation, stakeholder communication, sprint support, delivery coordination, and production troubleshooting.

Impact: The role strengthened my ability to work independently, adapt quickly, and take ownership beyond traditional QA boundaries whenever project success required it.
